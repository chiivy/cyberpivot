CyberPivot is built around one idea. That the best way to learn security is to actually do it. Not watch it. Not read about it. Do it. Every path on this platform produces real artifacts, real lab configurations, and real documents that you can show to an employer.

## How to Start

When you first arrive you go through a short assessment. Five or six questions depending on your answers. The questions are not a quiz. They are designed to understand where you are starting from and what kind of work interests you so the platform can point you in the right direction.

Based on your answers you get a recommendation. A role that fits your background and interests, an entry point into the content, and a preview of the cabinet artifacts you will build by the end. The recommendation is a starting point, not a verdict. You can change your path at any time, start at a different module if you already have relevant knowledge, or skip straight to a specialist module if you have a specific gap to fill.

If you already work in security, go straight to your chosen role path or a specialist module. The onboarding assessment is designed for people figuring out where to start. If you already know, skip it.

## The Content Structure

CyberPivot has five levels of content.

**Introduction** is where you are now. Free, no account needed. Eight sections covering what cybersecurity is, how the industry works, what different roles look like, and what employers actually care about. Read it before committing to anything.

**Foundation modules** cover the technical baseline. Networking, Linux, Windows and Active Directory, security fundamentals, cloud fundamentals, and Python basics. If you are coming from an IT background you may already know much of this. If you are a complete beginner these modules are where you build the ground everything else stands on. Foundation modules are recommended before starting a role path if you need them. They are not a locked gate.

Ready to start? If you are a complete beginner, Foundation Module 1 is the right first step. [Start Foundation Module 1 →](/foundations/how-the-internet-works)

**Role paths** are the main learning journeys. Each path is a structured sequence of modules built around what practitioners in that role actually do on the job. Every module follows the same structure. A real scenario first, then the concept explained plainly, then hands-on work with real tools, then an introduction to the enterprise equivalent you will see on the job, then a cabinet artifact you produce and keep.

**Specialist modules** are deep dives into specific topics. Some are embedded inside role paths. All are available as standalone content. If you are already working in security and want to go deep on a specific area, go straight there.

**Topic library** is short focused explainers. One concept, explained clearly, with a practical angle. Community contributed and growing over time.

## The Cabinet

The cabinet is your portfolio. It is the most important feature on the platform.

Every module you complete produces a real artifact. A working SIEM deployment with documented detection rules. A professional penetration test report with real findings. An ISO 27001 gap analysis for a simulated organisation. A Postman collection of API security tests. These are not badges or completion certificates. They are work products that demonstrate you did the work.

When a hiring manager asks for evidence of your skills, you show them your cabinet. Not a list of courses you watched. Actual artifacts from actual work.

Cabinet items are locked until you complete the relevant module. As you progress through a path they unlock one by one. By the time you finish you have a complete set of artifacts that tell a coherent story about your capability in that role.

Your cabinet profile is shareable. You can send a link to an employer, a recruiter, or a mentor. It shows your chosen path, your progress, and every artifact you have produced.

## Progress and Continuity

You can explore CyberPivot without an account. Your onboarding answers and chosen path save locally in your browser.

To save your progress permanently and access it from any device, create a free account. Sign in with Google, GitHub, or email. Creating an account is never required to start learning. It becomes useful when you want to pick up from a different machine or share your profile.

## Interview Prep and the CV Builder

Each role path includes interview preparation specific to that role. Real questions that come up in security interviews, with context on what good answers look like. Technical questions, situational questions, and the questions about your background that career switchers often find hardest to answer well.

The CV builder helps you translate your cabinet artifacts into CV bullet points formatted for the role you are targeting. The templates are built for each role and structured to read naturally to a human reviewer while passing applicant tracking systems.

## How Long It Takes

There is no honest single answer. It depends on your starting point, how much time you invest, and how deep you go in the labs.

A complete beginner working through foundations and one full role path should expect six to twelve months of consistent effort alongside other commitments. Someone with an IT background moving into security can often complete a role path in three to six months. Someone already working in security filling a specific gap might finish relevant modules in a few weeks.

There are no deadlines. Progress at the pace that works for you. The cabinet artifacts you produce are what matter. Not how quickly you produce them.

## Contributing to CyberPivot

CyberPivot is built with community contributions in mind. The platform is on GitHub.

The easiest way to contribute is through the Topic Library. Write a focused explainer on any cybersecurity topic, follow the template in the repository, submit a pull request. If it meets the quality standard it gets merged and you are credited on the platform.

For module content, check the GitHub issues for modules tagged content-needed. Each one has a brief explaining what the module should cover and a template to follow. If you have expertise in a specific area and want to help someone else build a career in it, that is where to start.

Contributors are credited in the platform and in the repository.

## If It Helped You, Tell Someone

If CyberPivot helps you land a role, we want to know. Not for vanity metrics. Because it validates that the approach works and helps us improve what we build next.

Tag us on social media when you get hired. Share your cabinet profile with the community. Tell the person in your network who is trying to break into security that this exists. Word of mouth from someone who actually used the platform and got a result is more valuable than any marketing we could do.

The platform grows when the people it helps bring others to it. If that is you, thank you in advance.

## A Final Note

CyberPivot exists because the gap between cybersecurity education and cybersecurity employment is real and damaging. People spend money on courses that do not prepare them for jobs. Employers spend money on recruitment and then months waiting for people to become productive. Entry level candidates struggle to get experience without a job and cannot get a job without experience.

This platform is an attempt to bridge that gap. Real content, real tools, real artifacts, honest career guidance. The measure of whether it works is not how many people sign up. It is how many people land a job they would not have landed otherwise.

If you go through a path on CyberPivot and it helps you get hired, that is the point. Everything else is in service of that.
